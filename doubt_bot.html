Can you explain the difference between intrinsic and extraneous cognitive load?

smart_toy
Source: Page 4

Based on the provided text, the primary difference lies in the source of the load relative to the learning task:

  • Intrinsic Cognitive Load: This is the inherent difficulty associated with a specific instructional topic. It is determined by the "element interactivity" of the task. For example, learning simple vocabulary has lower intrinsic load than learning complex grammatical structures.
  • Extraneous Cognitive Load: This refers to load that does not contribute to learning. It is created by the way information is presented. Poor instructional design (like a confusing layout or irrelevant animations) increases this load, taking up mental resources that should be used for learning.

The goal of effective pedagogical design is to manage intrinsic load while minimizing extraneous load.

Ref: Sweller (1988)
